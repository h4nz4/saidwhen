## 1. RECOMMENDATION

**Don't build it.** "Sign in with Google" was already evaluated and explicitly **rejected** in an accepted decision dated today (2026-07-08), in favor of magic-link auth. The evidence is fresh (same-day), so I'm respecting it, not re-litigating.

Rationale on record: users are external freelancers with **no corporate IdP** to SSO against, and each OAuth provider adds console setup, credential rotation, and breakage surface a **solo maintainer** (a few hrs/week) can't absorb. Magic links already deliver passwordless sign-in with near-zero ops.

The decision names its own reopen condition: *"Revisit if a significant user segment demands social login."* That's the only thing worth checking before spending effort here — see QUESTIONS.

## 3. QUESTIONS

Only the delta that could reopen the decision:

1. **What changed?** The reject was conditional on "revisit if a significant user segment demands social login." Is there now evidence of that demand — support requests, churn, a specific customer — or is this a general "nice to have"?
2. **Google specifically, or SSO in general?** If it's a real corporate customer arriving, that contradicts the external-freelancer constraint and would need a constraint update, not just a feature.
3. **Who absorbs the ongoing ops?** OAuth console config, consent-screen verification, and token/secret rotation land on the solo maintainer. Has that changed, or does magic-link + Google both need supporting indefinitely?

If the answer to #1 is "no concrete demand yet," the lazy call stands: keep magic links, revisit when a real segment shows up.
