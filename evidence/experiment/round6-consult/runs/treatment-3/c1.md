1. CONSTRAINTS AND WHY

- PocketLedger must remain a plain static single-page app using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.  
  Why: the owner wants a personal tool with no build step, framework, dependencies, backend, server, accounts, or operations burden.

- The app must work by opening `index.html` directly from disk.  
  Why: this preserves the owner’s no-server, no-operations requirement.

- Expense data must stay in browser `localStorage`.  
  Why: the app is for one person and should not require a backend, server, account system, sync service, or remote database.

- Expenses are EUR-only.  
  Why: the owner only spends in EUR; multi-currency was explicitly rejected unless that changes.

- The core workflow is intentionally small: add expenses with amount, category, date, and note; list expenses; delete expenses; show the current month’s total.  
  Why: this was chosen as the smallest useful personal expense-tracking workflow.

- Category support includes filtering the expense list by category and showing current-month totals grouped by category.  
  Why: the owner explicitly requested category filtering and per-category totals for the current month.

- CSV export must download all stored expenses, not just the active filtered view.  
  Why: the owner asked for a button that downloads all expenses as CSV, and browser-generated export preserves the static local architecture.

- Expanded finance features are out of scope unless a concrete repeated need appears.  
  Why: charts, reports, accounts, sync, multi-currency, cross-month category reports, external reporting services, and filtered-only CSV exports were rejected to keep the app small and boring for spare-time maintenance.

3. OPEN QUESTIONS

- The project has no formal entries under `## Constraints` in `docs/knowledge/index.md`; the binding constraints are recorded as accepted decisions instead.
- The wiki does not state whether future maintainers should keep everything in one physical file or may split static files, as long as the app remains disk-openable and dependency-free.
- The wiki does not define CSV column order, date format, escaping rules, or filename requirements.
- The wiki does not define allowed category names, whether categories are free-form, or whether categories should be editable/managed separately.
