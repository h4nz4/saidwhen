## 1. RECOMMENDATION

**Do not build it.** "Sign in with Google" OAuth SSO was already evaluated and explicitly **rejected today** (2026-07-08) in an accepted decision. The evidence is same-day fresh, so there's no staleness delta to re-open — I'm respecting it, not re-litigating.

Three grounds, all recorded:
- **No corporate IdP to attach to.** Users are individual freelancers, not org employees — there's no Google Workspace/Okta/Entra tenant SSO is meant to ride on. Google OAuth would just be a consumer-Gmail login button, not real SSO.
- **Operational surface a solo maintainer can't carry.** Each OAuth provider adds console setup, credential rotation, and a new breakage path. The maintainer works a few hours/week and set the bar at "anything that can break at 3am is out."
- **Magic-link already covers the need** with near-zero maintenance and no password/liability exposure.

The recorded decision leaves one door open: *"Revisit if a significant user segment demands social login."* That's a demand-driven trigger, not a build-now signal. Bring evidence of that demand (see §3) and it gets reopened — otherwise this is settled.
