## 1. RECOMMENDATION

**Don't build it.** This was already evaluated and explicitly rejected today (2026-07-08) in an accepted decision. Nothing in the PM's request changes the evidence, so I'm respecting the decision rather than re-litigating it.

"Sign in with Google" was rejected because TaskLite's users are external freelancers with no corporate IdP (so SSO buys them nothing), and each OAuth provider adds console setup, credential rotation, and a breakage surface that a solo, few-hours-a-week maintainer can't absorb. The shipped answer — single-use magic links — covers the actual need (passwordless login, no password-storage liability) at near-zero maintenance.

The recorded revisit trigger is specific: *"a significant user segment demands social login."* The PM request doesn't assert that — it's a "let's add it" proposal, not evidence of user demand. So the bar to reopen hasn't been met.

## 3. QUESTIONS

I'm *not* asking the questions the wiki already answers (who the users are, SSO need, maintenance budget, compliance). The only thing that could reopen this:

1. **Is there new evidence of user demand?** The decision says revisit "if a significant user segment demands social login." Are freelancers actually asking for Google sign-in, or is this a proposal from the PM's side? If the former — how many, and how are we measuring it?
2. **If demand is real, what's the accepted maintenance cost?** The blocker isn't the login UX, it's the ongoing ops (OAuth console, secret rotation, provider breakage) landing on a solo maintainer. Who owns that at 3am?

Absent a "yes" with evidence on #1, my recommendation is to keep magic links and close this out.
